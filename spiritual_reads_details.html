Grand Nagar Yatra (Procession) Launches New Mandir Inauguration
Festivities

12 Jan 2020 02:00PM

A grand procession through the main streets of Navsari city marked the start of the three-day festivities to commemorate the consecration of the murtis in the new BAPS Swaminarayan Mandir.

Stretching for 1.5 km, the procession consisted of over 60 creative attractions, including beautifully decorated chariots housing the various murtis,

youths on horseback and motorcycles, children and youths performing a variety of traditional dances, BAPS sadhus and youths singing devotional bhajans, tribal youths playing drums, youths carrying lighted torches (mashals), women devotees carrying kalashes (auspicious water pots) and other presentations.

The participation of over 300 BAPS sadhus and 4,000 devotees from Gujarat and abroad enhanced the devout atmosphere created by the procession.

Thousands of local residents joyously and devoutly welcomed the procession along the 4 km route by showering flower petals on and offering garlands to the murtis. The murtis cast their divine gaze upon the city to bless all the residents.

Senior BAPS sadhus and Shri Piyushbhai Desai (MLA, Navsari) performed the initial pujan rituals to flag off the procession.

The procession set off from the Garda College Hostel grounds at 2.30 p.m. and concluded at the BAPS Swaminarayan Mandir at 7.00 p.m. in the presence of Mahant Swami Maharaj.
